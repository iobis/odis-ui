ODIS / Ocean InfoHub — search interface concepts

Faceted search, four ways

Four layout directions for browsing federated ocean data, literature, organizations and training material across the OIH graph. Spatial extent is treated as a per-record detail — relevant when a result is a dataset with a bounding box — rather than a map-first experience.

01

The log

A dense, sortable register modelled on a ship's log or spreadsheet rather than a card feed. Facets sit as compact dropdown buttons in the toolbar instead of a sidebar, so the record table gets full width. Built for data managers scanning and comparing many records at once — extent only appears where it applies.

02

The catalog

Facets become a horizontal pill bar — closer to a museum collection browser than a filter form — freeing the full canvas for a browsable card grid. Best suited to public-facing discovery, where people scan and skim rather than cross-reference precise fields. Extent is a quiet footer detail on dataset cards only.

03

The research rail

The familiar sidebar-facets pattern, refined: collapsible facet groups with a time-range slider, active filters as coloured chips, and a lightweight result list rather than heavy cards. The safe, conventional option — closest to what GBIF or OBIS's own mapper users already know.

The split explorer

A mail-client pattern: slim facet rail, compact result list, and a detail pane that opens on selection. This is where spatial extent earns real estate — shown as a small bounding-box readout inside the detail pane for datasets, never as a global map. Good for triaging many similar records without leaving the page. Click a row to try it.

ConceptDensityBest fitSpatial extent treatment
01 · The logVery highData managers, cross-record comparisonCompact icon + coordinates, its own column
02 · The catalogLowPublic discovery, casual browsingSmall footer detail on dataset cards
03 · The research railMediumGeneral search, broad audienceInline meta line, dataset rows only
04 · The split explorerMedium-highTriage across many similar recordsDedicated block in the detail pane

In every concept, extent is scoped to individual dataset records rather than a persistent map view — literature, organizations, training material and projects simply omit it. A full map only makes sense as a secondary view (e.g. "view on map" from the extent block in concept 04), not as the primary way into the catalog.
